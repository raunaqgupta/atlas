When two or more [[User]]s are working a shared [[Task]], they need to exchange context — questions, status updates, decisions — without losing it to a separate chat channel. The job is to keep the conversation tethered to the artifact it's about, so the trail survives after the conversation ends.

## Tasks

1. Open a [[Task]] that has another [[User]] involved.
2. Author a [[Comment]] with the question, update, or decision.
3. The other [[User]] receives a notification (via [[Reminder]] or external channel) and replies with another [[Comment]].
4. Resolve the thread by updating the [[Task]] itself (status, due date, assignee) — the comment thread remains as the audit trail.

## Success criteria

- The full back-and-forth is reconstructable from the task page alone.
- New people brought onto the task can read the comment thread and get up to speed without asking.
